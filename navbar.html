How to Plant Macadamia Nuts

You often start by harvesting them from the farm. You then dip them in water overnight or for a maximum of three days. You will notice that some will float while others will sink. Those that will float wont germinate. It is thus advised that you dispose them away.

After this you should place them to dry in the sun to enable cracking of the hard shell. You will notice that some of the Macadamia will crack while others will remain uncracked.Select those that have cracked from those that have not craacked. You should then proceed to hit them a little with a hammer to get a small crack. After this, you are good to go to transplant them either in sand or loam soil. I prefer sand for better rooting system and to do away with pests and other forms of attacks.You should take note on how you lay them on the ground for a maximum harvest. The area with a scar should be laid down so that the radicle can be able to form properly.The next stage is to cover the Macadamia with a little layer of soil. Ensure that your Nursery bed is always moist. You can sit down, relax and wait for the Macadamia to shoot in a maximum of one month and 3 weeks.
